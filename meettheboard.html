The CSA Board (2024 - 2025)

Jett Kletter (2021) - President

Why CSA? I joined CSA mainly as a way to give back to the broader Chancellor’s Scholarship Program and improve myself along the way. I am far from being the most extroverted person and had not really gone into event planning, public speaking, hosting events and delegating tasks as a leader before CSA, which means that CSA has been a great first step into making me go beyond my comfort zone. Not to mention, the Chancellor’s Scholarship Program has provided me with a litany of opportunities and being able to give a little back to CSP in the form of social events, volunteering and more, fostering a stronger community is also rewarding.

Carlos Gomez (2022) - Webmaster

Why CSA? As a first year, the CSA did a great job of keeping us involved. Therefore, I knew I wanted to join CSA in order to contribute to fun events and to create a more close knit community.

Keene Cheung (2022) - Social Chair

Why CSA? During my first year as a Chancellor’s Scholar, I have been able to meet many new people through this program who have helped me find my way through college and succeed. I decided to join the board because I wanted to be able to give back to the program and help create a tighter community within our program in the future.

Foster Dang (2022) - Social Chair

Why CSA? In my freshman year (so last year for me), I attended a lot of the CSA events and found the community to be extremely kind and welcoming. By doing (co)social chair, I just wanted to pay it forward and ensure that the program is as welcoming this year as it was last year. I encourage you guys to come to our events, I promise we won’t bite. :)

Kristilynn Effie (2022) - Secretary

Why CSA? I learned by being an active member and on the board of the Chancellor's Scholars Alliance, I would be able to welcome new members, gain leadership experience, and be a part of a community that shares the same values as I do. It excited me to join the CSA board where I would also be given the opportunity of a platform and make impactful decisions, as well as network with influential mentors and other individuals later in life.

Desiree Menjivar (2022) - Peer Mentoring

Why CSA? I decided to join the CSA Board because I've always appreciated the effort it took to coordinate events for the CSP students. As someone with experience on executive boards, I wanted to apply my skills here and offer as much help as possible! Additionally, I enjoy the feeling of closeness when working in a small group, so I hope to strengthen the relationship with my colleagues.

Carlynda Ng (2022) - Volunteer

Why CSA? I decided to join CSA because I wanted to create a community for the Chancellor Scholars where they can give back to the community and de-stress. I also wanted to join the CSA board to interact with other cohorts and learn from my peers.

Sarah Griffith (2020) - Treasurer

Why CSA? The community aspect of CSP was always something that I really appreciated during my first years at UCSD, even though the 2020 cohort did not have the opportunity to meet in-person very often. Because of this, I wanted to be a part of the CSA board both to help foster a sense of community for the incoming cohorts and to remain connected to the program and all the great people within it.

Reynelle Aricheta (2022) - Peer Mentoring
